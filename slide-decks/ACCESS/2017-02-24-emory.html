Additional Links

  • "Access" Digital Pedagogy in the Humanities (MLA Commons): A curated set of links to online resources. "The artifacts collected here were selected with three different activities central to teaching and learning in mind: planning, evaluating, and re-thinking."
  • "Access Guidelines for MLA Convention Session Organizers and Presenters" Modern Language Association Committee on Disability Issues in the Profession: Advice for making your session accessible to audience members and participants. Useful for thinking about teaching.
  • UDL on Campus: A site devoted to universal design for learning in higher education.
  • Accessible Syllabus: Advice on creating "accessible classroom resources [to] promote student engagement and agency."
  • "Cheatsheets" from the National Center on Disability and Access to Education: "These one-page accessibility resources, or 'cheatsheets,' have been developed to assist anyone who is creating accessible content. These free resources are catered to less-technical individuals, such as faculty and staff."
  • WebAIM Articles: These articles cover a wide range of topics from creating accessible web pages to thinking through alternative text for images to captioning videos.
